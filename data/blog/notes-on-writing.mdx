I use a few rules when I write about work:

- Keep the intro short.
- State the problem before describing the solution.
- Include enough detail that I can reuse the note later.
- Avoid pretending every experiment is a finished pattern.

The best technical notes usually answer one of three questions:

1. What happened?
2. Why did it happen?
3. What would I do differently next time?

If a draft does not help with one of those questions, it is probably too vague. I would rather publish something small and specific than something broad that is hard to use again.
